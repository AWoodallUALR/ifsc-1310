Thoughts on Web Accessibility

It is important to build accessible websites because technology is increasingly a requirement of participating in modern society. Options that do not require the internet are disappearing in many cases, and this can lock people out of important services that they need. Employment, healthcare, government services, utilities, financial services, the list of important areas where internet access is involved and even required is endless. Designing websites that work with screen readers, can be navigated using keyboard or alternatives and that are descriptive and simple to navigate helps people. Imagine struggling with a limitation and attempting to do something as simple as paying your bills. Particularly if you canâ€™t leave home on your own, accessibility is the difference between being able to take care of a simple task independently and having to contact someone else and have them do it for you. A lack of accessible options can make people feel like the world is not designed for them and makes what may already be a very frustrating situation even harder. Making websites useable for the greatest number of people helps makes things as equitable as possible and gives those who may be disabled permanently or temporarily or challenged in some way greater autonomy and control over their lives.
